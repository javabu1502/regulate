# Regulate — Meditation Narration Scripts

Voice direction: Warm, slow, unhurried. Like you're sitting next to someone you care about.
Generous pauses between sentences — marked with `[pause]` (2-3 sec) and `[long pause]` (5-8 sec).
No forced cheerfulness. No toxic positivity. Body-first, somatic, grounding.

---

## 1. Safe Place (~5 min)
**File:** `meditations/safe-place.mp3`
**Description:** Build a mental space that feels safe

Let's find a place where nothing is asked of you.

[pause]

Close your eyes if that feels okay. If not, soften your gaze and let it rest somewhere low.

[pause]

Take a slow breath in. And let it go.

[long pause]

I want you to think of a place — real or imagined — where you feel safe. It doesn't have to be perfect. It just has to feel like yours.

[pause]

Maybe it's a room you know well. A corner of a house. A spot outside where the light hits a certain way. Maybe it's somewhere you've never been — somewhere your mind invented just for you.

[long pause]

Start to see it. What's around you? Don't rush. Just let the details arrive.

[pause]

What colors do you notice? What's the light like — soft? Warm? Dim?

[long pause]

Now notice the ground beneath you. Are you sitting? Standing? Lying down? Whatever feels right.

[pause]

Feel the surface under you. Is it soft? Firm? Cool? Warm? Let your body settle into it.

[long pause]

What do you hear in this place? Maybe nothing at all. Maybe something quiet — wind, water, the hum of a room that feels lived in.

[long pause]

Take a breath and notice how the air feels here. Is there a smell? Something familiar? Something comforting?

[pause]

This place is yours. No one can come here unless you invite them. Nothing bad happens here.

[long pause]

Let your shoulders drop. Let your belly soften. You don't have to hold anything right now.

[pause]

If your mind wanders, that's fine. Just come back to this place. It's still here.

[long pause]

Take a moment to look around one more time. Notice one thing you want to remember about this place. A detail that makes it yours.

[long pause]

Now take a slow breath in. And a slow breath out.

[pause]

This place lives in you. You can come back whenever you need it. It doesn't go away when you open your eyes.

[pause]

When you're ready — there's no rush — start to notice the room you're actually in. The sounds around you. The feel of whatever's beneath you.

[pause]

Slowly open your eyes. You're here. And your safe place is still there, waiting for you.

[long pause]

## 2. Body as Home (~6 min)
**File:** `meditations/body-as-home.mp3`
**Description:** Reconnecting with your body

Sometimes your body doesn't feel like a safe place to be. That's okay. We're not going to force anything. We're just going to visit — gently — and see what's there.

[pause]

Close your eyes or soften your gaze. Take a breath. No need to change it — just notice it happening.

[long pause]

Start with your feet. Can you feel them? The weight of them. The way they connect to the ground. You don't need to do anything with them. Just notice they're there.

[long pause]

Now your legs. The weight of your thighs. Your knees. Are they holding tension? If they are, that's okay. You're just noticing.

[pause]

Move up to your hips. Your lower back. This is where a lot of us carry things — stress, grief, exhaustion. You don't have to fix it. Just say hello to it.

[long pause]

Now your belly. Let it be soft if it can. If it can't, that's information too. Your belly holds a lot of your emotional world. Thank it for that.

[pause]

Your chest. Your heart. Can you feel it beating? That rhythm has been with you since before you were born. It hasn't stopped. Not once.

[long pause]

Your shoulders. Your neck. If there's tension here, imagine it like a fist that's been clenched for a long time. You're not prying it open. You're just letting it know it can loosen if it wants to.

[long pause]

Your face. Your jaw. Your forehead. Let everything soften. Even a little.

[pause]

Your whole body is here, right now, holding you. It's been doing this your entire life. Through everything.

[long pause]

Take a moment to just... be in your body. Not thinking about it. Not analyzing it. Just being in it. Like sitting in a room you haven't visited in a while.

[long pause]

Your body is not your enemy. Even when it doesn't feel good, it's doing its best to take care of you. It's always been on your side.

[pause]

Take a slow, deep breath. Feel your chest rise. Feel it fall.

[pause]

And again.

[long pause]

When you're ready, start to move your fingers. Your toes. Small movements, like you're waking up gently.

[pause]

Open your eyes when it feels right. You just came home, even if only for a few minutes. That matters.

[long pause]

## 3. Feet on the Floor (~3 min)
**File:** `meditations/feet-on-the-floor.mp3`
**Description:** Quick present-moment anchoring

This one is short. You don't need to go anywhere or do anything special. Just be where you are.

[pause]

Feel your feet on the floor. Press them down a little. Feel the ground pushing back.

[long pause]

That's real. The floor is real. You're real. This moment is real.

[pause]

Take a breath in. Let it out slowly.

[pause]

Now press your feet down again. Feel your heels. The balls of your feet. Your toes. All of it connecting to something solid.

[long pause]

Notice the temperature of the floor through your shoes or socks. Or if your feet are bare, notice how the surface feels. Cool? Warm? Smooth? Rough?

[pause]

You are here. Not in the past. Not in the future. Here. With your feet on the floor.

[long pause]

Now notice what's holding the rest of you. A chair. A bed. The ground. Something is supporting your weight right now. Let it.

[pause]

You don't have to hold yourself up. Something else is doing that for you.

[long pause]

Take one more slow breath. In through your nose. Out through your mouth.

[pause]

Feel your feet. Feel the ground. You're here. That's enough.

[long pause]

## 4. Letting the Wave Pass (~6 min)
**File:** `meditations/letting-the-wave-pass.mp3`
**Description:** Riding difficult emotions

Whatever you're feeling right now — it's a wave. And waves pass. Every single one.

[pause]

Close your eyes if you can. Take a breath. Don't try to calm down. Don't try to feel better. Just breathe.

[long pause]

I want you to notice what's happening in your body. Not your thoughts — your body. Where is the feeling living right now?

[pause]

Maybe it's in your chest. A tightness. A pressure. Maybe it's in your stomach — a churning, a knot. Maybe it's everywhere at once.

[long pause]

Whatever it is, put your hand there. Gently. Like you're touching something fragile.

[pause]

You don't have to understand it. You don't have to name it. You just have to stay with it.

[long pause]

Now imagine this feeling as a wave in the ocean. It's building. You can feel it rising. The intensity is growing.

[pause]

Your instinct is to fight it. To push it down. To run from it. But you're not going to do that. You're going to let it come.

[long pause]

Breathe into it. Not to make it stop. Just to give it space.

[pause]

The wave is rising. Let it. You've survived every wave before this one. Your body knows how to do this.

[long pause]

Notice the peak. The moment where it's the most intense. You're right here. You're in it. And you're still breathing.

[pause]

Now feel it start to come down. Even a little. That's the wave breaking.

[long pause]

It's passing. It's not gone — but it's moving. That's what waves do. They move through.

[pause]

Let your breath slow down if it wants to. Let your body soften if it can.

[long pause]

The water is settling. You're still here. You rode it out. You didn't break.

[pause]

Take a slow breath. In. And out.

[long pause]

The next wave might come. That's okay. Now you know — you know how to float until it passes.

[pause]

When you're ready, open your eyes. You're on the other side. You made it.

[long pause]

## 5. Putting It Down (~5 min)
**File:** `meditations/putting-it-down.mp3`
**Description:** Setting down what you're carrying

You've been carrying something. Maybe for hours. Maybe for years. Let's set it down — just for a few minutes.

[pause]

Close your eyes. Take a breath. Feel the weight of your body wherever you are.

[long pause]

Think about what you've been carrying. Not the details — just the weight of it. The heaviness. The way it sits on your shoulders, your chest, your mind.

[pause]

You don't have to solve it right now. You don't have to figure it out. You just have to set it down for a few minutes.

[long pause]

Imagine it as something you're physically holding. A bag. A box. A stone. Something heavy. Feel it in your hands.

[pause]

Now — very slowly — imagine placing it on the ground in front of you. Feel the weight leaving your hands. Your arms. Your shoulders.

[long pause]

It's still there. You're not throwing it away. You're not pretending it doesn't exist. You're just not holding it right now.

[pause]

Notice what your body feels like without it. Even a little lighter? A little more space in your chest?

[long pause]

Roll your shoulders back. Let them drop. Unclench your jaw.

[pause]

Breathe into the space that just opened up. Fill it with air instead of worry.

[long pause]

You are allowed to put things down. You are allowed to rest from the carrying. You don't have to earn that.

[pause]

Stay here for a moment. Just breathing. Just being a person who isn't holding anything.

[long pause]

When you're ready — and only when you're ready — you can pick it back up. But maybe a little differently this time. Maybe not all of it at once.

[pause]

Take a slow breath. Open your eyes. The weight is still there, but so is the knowledge that you can set it down. Anytime.

[long pause]

## 6. The Container (~5 min)
**File:** `meditations/the-container.mp3`
**Description:** Placing overwhelming thoughts somewhere safe

When there's too much in your head — too many thoughts, too many feelings, too much noise — it helps to have somewhere to put it. Not to get rid of it. Just to hold it for you.

[pause]

Close your eyes. Take a breath. Let it be slow.

[long pause]

I want you to imagine a container. It can be anything — a box, a chest, a vault, a jar, a room. Whatever feels right. Whatever feels strong enough to hold what you need to put in it.

[pause]

See it clearly. What does it look like? What's it made of? How big is it?

[long pause]

Now think about what's been overwhelming you. The thoughts that won't stop. The feelings that are too big. The things you keep turning over in your mind.

[pause]

You're going to place them in the container. One at a time.

[long pause]

Take the first thing. Don't analyze it. Don't relive it. Just notice it — and place it inside.

[pause]

Close the lid a little.

[pause]

Take the next thing. Place it inside.

[long pause]

Keep going. Whatever comes up. Place it in the container. You're not losing it. You're not ignoring it. You're putting it somewhere safe so your mind can rest.

[long pause]

When you've put in everything you need to, close the container. Seal it however feels right — a lock, a latch, a heavy lid.

[pause]

It's closed. Everything is in there. And it's secure.

[long pause]

Now take a breath. Notice how your mind feels with a little more space.

[pause]

You can open this container whenever you want. When you're ready. When you have support. When you choose to. But right now, it's closed. And that's okay.

[long pause]

Breathe. Your mind just got a little quieter. Let that be enough for now.

[pause]

Open your eyes when you're ready. The container is there whenever you need it.

[long pause]

## 7. The Younger You (~7 min)
**File:** `meditations/the-younger-you.mp3`
**Description:** Speaking kindly to your younger self

This one might bring up some feelings. That's okay. You're safe here.

[pause]

Close your eyes. Take a slow breath. Let your body settle.

[long pause]

I want you to think about yourself as a child. Not a specific memory — just you, small. However old feels right. Maybe five. Maybe eight. Maybe twelve.

[pause]

See that younger version of you. What are they wearing? Where are they? What do they look like?

[long pause]

Look at their face. What's there? Are they scared? Tired? Confused? Hopeful?

[pause]

This is you. This is who you were before everything got complicated. Before you learned to carry so much.

[long pause]

I want you to sit next to them. Not as the adult who has all the answers — because you don't. Just as someone who cares about them. Someone who knows what they've been through.

[pause]

What do they need to hear?

[long pause]

Maybe something like: It's not your fault.

[pause]

Or: You're going to be okay. I know it doesn't feel like it. But you are.

[long pause]

Or: You are not too much. You are not too little. You are exactly right.

[pause]

Or: I'm sorry no one said this to you sooner.

[long pause]

Whatever words come — let them come. Say them silently to that younger you. Mean them.

[long pause]

Now imagine putting your arm around them. Or holding their hand. Whatever feels right.

[pause]

They don't have to be brave right now. Neither do you.

[long pause]

Take a breath together.

[pause]

That kid grew up to be you. And you're here. Still going. Still trying. That says something.

[long pause]

Before you let the image go, tell them one more thing: I've got you now. You don't have to do this alone anymore.

[long pause]

Take a slow breath. Let the image fade gently. Don't rush it.

[pause]

When you open your eyes, you might feel tender. That's not weakness. That's your heart remembering how to be soft.

[pause]

Open your eyes. You're here. And so is that younger you — held, finally, by someone who understands.

[long pause]

## 8. You Did Your Best Today (~5 min)
**File:** `meditations/you-did-your-best-today.mp3`
**Description:** End-of-day wind down

The day is done. Whatever happened — it's over. You made it through.

[pause]

Close your eyes. Let your body be heavy. You don't have to hold yourself up anymore.

[long pause]

Take a breath. A real one. Deep into your belly. And let it all the way out.

[pause]

Again. In. And out. Let it sound like a sigh if it wants to.

[long pause]

I want you to let go of the idea that you should have done more today. You did what you could with what you had. That's it. That's enough.

[pause]

Maybe the day was hard. Maybe you didn't do everything on your list. Maybe you barely got through it. That still counts.

[long pause]

Think of one thing you did today. It doesn't have to be impressive. Maybe you got out of bed. Maybe you fed yourself. Maybe you answered a message you'd been avoiding. Maybe you just kept breathing when everything felt heavy.

[pause]

That matters. All of it matters.

[long pause]

Now let the day go. You don't need to review it. You don't need to grade yourself. It's done.

[pause]

Let your shoulders release. Let your face soften. Let your jaw drop open just a little.

[long pause]

Feel the surface beneath you. It's holding you. You can let go of your weight. All of it.

[pause]

You did your best today. Even if your best looked different from what you wanted, it was enough. You are enough.

[long pause]

Take one more slow breath. In. And out.

[pause]

Tomorrow is tomorrow. Right now, you're just a person resting. And that's exactly what you should be doing.

[long pause]

## 9. Permission to Rest (~5 min)
**File:** `meditations/permission-to-rest.mp3`
**Description:** For people who feel guilty resting

If you're listening to this, part of you probably feels like you shouldn't be. Like there's something else you should be doing. Something more productive. More useful.

[pause]

I want to say something clearly: You are allowed to rest.

[pause]

Close your eyes. Take a breath. And hear that again.

[pause]

You are allowed to rest. Not because you earned it. Not because you finished everything. Just because you're a human being and human beings need rest.

[long pause]

Notice the voice in your head that says otherwise. The one that says you're lazy. The one that says other people have it harder. The one that says you should be able to handle more.

[pause]

That voice learned to say those things somewhere. It's not the truth. It's a pattern.

[long pause]

Right now, in this moment, nothing is required of you. Nothing.

[pause]

Let your body get heavy. Let your muscles stop working so hard. They've been holding you up — literally — all day.

[long pause]

Your jaw. Unclench it. Your hands. Open them. Your stomach. Let it be soft.

[pause]

Breathing in. Breathing out. That's all you have to do.

[long pause]

Resting is not quitting. Resting is not weakness. Resting is how your body repairs. How your nervous system recalibrates. How you come back stronger.

[pause]

You wouldn't tell someone you love that they don't deserve rest. So don't tell yourself that either.

[long pause]

Stay here. Just breathing. Just resting. As long as you need.

[pause]

When you're ready to come back, do it slowly. There's no rush. The world will still be there. And you'll meet it a little more whole than you were before.

[long pause]

## 10. Warming Your Hands (~5 min)
**File:** `meditations/warming-your-hands.mp3`
**Description:** Guided body warming

This is a simple one. We're going to warm your hands using nothing but your attention.

[pause]

Close your eyes. Rest your hands on your lap or by your sides, palms facing up.

[pause]

Take a slow breath. Let your body settle.

[long pause]

Bring all your attention to your right hand. Just your right hand. Notice what it feels like. The weight of it. The temperature.

[pause]

Now imagine warmth. Like your hand is resting near a fire. Or holding a warm mug. Or sitting in a patch of sunlight.

[long pause]

Don't force it. Just imagine it. Let the warmth come on its own.

[pause]

Notice any tingling. Any heaviness. Any warmth — even a little. That's your blood flow increasing. That's your body responding to your attention.

[long pause]

Now bring that same attention to your left hand. Feel its weight. Its temperature.

[pause]

Imagine the warmth spreading there too. Both hands now, heavy and warm. Like they're glowing.

[long pause]

When your hands warm up, your nervous system shifts. Blood moves away from your muscles — away from fight-or-flight — and toward your core. Your body reads warmth as safety.

[pause]

Let the warmth spread up your arms. Into your chest. Down into your belly.

[long pause]

Your whole center, warm and heavy. Like you've been sitting in the sun.

[pause]

Stay here. Breathing slowly. Feeling the warmth.

[long pause]

This is your body calming itself down. No effort needed. Just attention and warmth.

[pause]

When you're ready, wiggle your fingers. Take a slow breath. Open your eyes. Notice if your hands are actually warmer than when you started. For most people, they are.

[long pause]

My right arm is heavy.

My right arm is heavy and warm.

[pause]

My left arm is heavy and warm.

[long pause]

Both arms now. My arms are heavy and warm.

Let them sink into whatever's beneath them. Like they're made of something dense and soft.

[pause]

My right leg is heavy and warm.

## 12. Following the Breath Home (~5 min)
**File:** `meditations/following-the-breath-home.mp3`
**Description:** Breath awareness without control

This is the simplest meditation there is. You're not going to change your breathing. You're just going to follow it.

[pause]

Close your eyes. Let your hands rest. Let your body be still.

[long pause]

Notice your breath. Don't change it. Don't make it deeper or slower. Just notice it. Like watching someone else breathe.

[pause]

Where do you feel it? Your nose? Your chest? Your belly? Find where it's most obvious and rest your attention there.

[long pause]

Breathing in. You know you're breathing in.

[pause]

Breathing out. You know you're breathing out.

[long pause]

That's it. That's the whole practice.

[pause]

Your mind is going to wander. That's not a failure — that's just what minds do. When you notice it's wandered, come back to the breath. No judgment. Just come back.

[long pause]

Breathing in.

[long pause]

Breathing out.

[long pause]

Notice the little pause at the top of each inhale. And the pause at the bottom of each exhale. Your body does this on its own. It knows the rhythm.

[long pause]

You don't have to breathe. Breathing is happening. You're just watching.

[long pause]

If your breath changes on its own — gets deeper, gets slower — let it. You're not controlling this. You're following.

[long pause]

Stay here. Following each breath like you're following a path home. In. Out. In. Out.

[long pause]

Your breath has been with you since the first moment of your life. It's the most reliable thing you have. It's always here.

[pause]

Take one more conscious breath. Feel it all the way in. Feel it all the way out.

[pause]

Open your eyes. You're here. You followed your breath home.

[long pause]

## 15. Rain on the Roof (~8 min)
**File:** `meditations/rain-on-the-roof.mp3`
**Description:** Safe shelter, letting go

Imagine you're somewhere warm and dry. A small room. Maybe a cabin. Maybe an attic. Maybe just a bed near a window. You're inside, and you're safe.

[pause]

Close your eyes. Take a breath. Let it go slowly.

[long pause]

And outside — it starts to rain.

[pause]

Softly at first. Just a few drops on the roof. Tap. Tap. Tap.

[long pause]

Then a little more. A steady, gentle rhythm. Not a storm — just rain. The kind that makes you want to curl up and stay exactly where you are.

[long pause]

Listen to it. The way it sounds on the roof above you. Consistent. Steady. Like it's saying: Stay here. There's nowhere else you need to be.

[long pause]

Feel the warmth of where you are. Maybe you're under a blanket. Maybe the room is just the right temperature. Whatever makes you feel held.

[pause]

The rain picks up a little. You can hear it hitting different surfaces. The roof. A window. Maybe a gutter or a ledge. Each one a different tone.

[long pause]

There's something about rain that gives your mind permission to stop. The world outside is muffled. Slowed down. It's like the rain is washing everything clean — not just the ground, but the noise in your head too.

[long pause]

Take a deep breath. Smell the rain, even in your imagination. That clean, earthy smell. Petrichor. The smell of something being made new.

[long pause]
